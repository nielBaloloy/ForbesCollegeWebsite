College of Information Technology Education

Overview

The BS Information Technology program covers the use of hardware and software technologies in the areas of planning, setting up, modifying, running, overseeing, and maintaining information technology infrastructure that offers computing solutions to meet an organization's needs.
The program prepares graduates to address various user needs involving the selection, development, application, integration and management of computing technologies within an organization.

Career Paths
  • Software Developer
  • Web Developer
  • Database Administrator
  • Network Engineer
  • Systems Analyst
  • UX UI Designer

Program Outcome

Graduates are expected to become globally competent, innovative, and socially and ethically responsible computing professionals engaged in life-long learning endeavours. They are capable of contributing to the country's national development goals.

College of Criminal Justice Education

Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Criminology or Criminal Justice is a four-year degree program intended for individuals who wish to have a career in the fields of law enforcement, security administration, crime detection, and prevention of correctional administration.

Career Paths
  • Criminological Research
  • Scientific Crime Detection and Investigation
  • Correctional Administration
  • Public Safety
  • Security Management
  • Criminalistics
  • Academe

Program Outcome

The program teaches students the various theories, policies, practices and laws associated with criminal behavior and the methods applied to manage deviant activities and correctional administration.
